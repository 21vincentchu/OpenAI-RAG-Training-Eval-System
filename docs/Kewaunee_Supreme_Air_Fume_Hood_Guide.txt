This document describes the operation, maintenance, and safety procedures for the Supreme Air Series Fume Hood, Model HOM-1107, which is actively used in our semiconductor lab.

Comprehensive Technical Guide: Kewaunee Supreme Air Series Fume Hoods
Fundamental Safety and Operational Procedures
Critical Operational Guidelines
Primary Operating Procedures
Perchloric Acid Restrictions
STRICTLY PROHIBITED: Using perchloric acid in non-specialized hoods
Severe explosion hazard with organic materials
Dedicated perchloric acid hoods required for such procedures
Pre-Operational Verification
Mandatory exhaust fan functionality check
Verify sufficient air exhaust volume
Confirm air flow integrity before each use
Contaminate Handling Protocols
Positioning Requirements:
All apparatus minimum 6 inches from sash opening
Place in recessed portion of work surface
Absolute Prohibition:
Never place head inside hood during contaminant generation
Avoid rapid movements near hood
Minimize personnel traffic around hood
Equipment Placement Specifications
Large Equipment Guidelines:
Use 2-3 inch tall metal legs for large apparatus
Ensures airflow circulation underneath
Prevents air flow obstruction
Surface Orientation:
Large flat surfaces parallel to hood face
Legs required to facilitate air movement
Electrical and Ignition Safety
Flammable Material Precautions:
No electrical receptacles near flammable substances
Mandatory safety shield for potential explosion risks
Hood NOT designed for comprehensive explosion protection
Protective Air Barrier Mechanics
Air Flow Characteristics
Pressure Zone Dynamics
Air passing body creates low-pressure zone
Extends approximately 4 inches into hood interior
Turbulent area susceptible to contaminant infiltration
Contaminant Containment Strategy
Recommended Placement:
Minimum 6 inches behind protective air barrier
Maximize distance from hood face
Air Velocity Parameters:
Typical containment velocity: 100 feet per minute
Easily disrupted air flow patterns
Sash Management Protocols
Opening Guidelines
Minimal Required Opening
Access only necessary work area
Reduce exposure to external air disturbances
Breathing Zone Protection:
Lowered sash increases distance from contaminant escape zone
Reduces susceptibility to room drafts
Sash Position Considerations
Horizontal Sash Requirements:
All sashes must be in place during operations
Removing sashes reduces air entry velocity
Height Limitations:
Respect marked safe sash height
Do not override sash stop mechanism
Baffle Configuration Systems
Baffle Type Descriptions
Configuration Options
Fixed Baffle Configuration
Optimized slot sizes for general use
Predetermined air flow characteristics
Internally Adjustable Baffles
Manual upper/lower slot size adjustment
Customizable air flow patterns
Internal Single Point Adjustable
Rotational damper mechanism
Precise air flow control
Baffle Position Strategies
Position A (Standard Operation):
Balanced air distribution
Uniform lower and upper hood area flow
Ideal for most standard laboratory procedures
Position B (High-Temperature Scenarios):
Enhanced upper exhaust volume
Optimal for large hot plate use
Manages rising thermal contaminants
Position C (Dense Vapor Handling):
Increased lower area air draw
Specialized for heavy gases and sinking vapors
Perchloric Acid Hood Specialized Procedures
Unique Operational Requirements
Dedicated Use Mandate
Exclusive use for perchloric acid procedures
Prevent cross-contamination risks
Wash-Down Protocol
Mandatory Water Wash Procedure:
15-minute wash after each use
End-of-day wash for continuous operation
Preparation Steps:
Clear work area of potentially damageable equipment
Anticipate potential water dripping
Exhaust System Management
Wash-Down Specific Instructions:
TURN OFF exhaust fan during water wash
Prevent impeller wheel water damage
Valve Location:
Left front vertical fascia
Potential separate duct activation switch
Maintenance Specifics
Weekly Interior Cleaning
Water-based cleaning protocol
Liner Preservation:
Monitor inorganic acid interactions
Potentially increased cleaning frequency
